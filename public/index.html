About the Project

Basic Information

Kwun Tong being one of the poorest districts in Hong Kong, yet filled with shopping malls and luxury brands, creates a striking contradiction.

「朱門酒肉臭，路有凍死骨」

"Behind vermilion gates, wine and meat rot; outside lie the frozen bones of the poor."

Roughly translates to "While the rich waste food inside their luxurious homes, the poor suffer and die of hunger and cold outside." It's a vivid metaphor for inequality and social injustice.

Objective

Construct a sequence of images to critically examine the coexistence of prosperity and poverty in Kwun Tong.

Aim

To critically examine and expose the stark socio-economic paradox of gentrification within Kwun Tong, visually tracking the friction between hyper-modern corporate wealth and deep-rooted grassroots deprivation.
